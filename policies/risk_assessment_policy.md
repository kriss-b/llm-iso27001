# Risk Assessment Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes a framework for identifying, evaluating, and mitigating risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets in alignment with **ISO 27001:2022 (Clause 6.1 — Actions to address risks and opportunities, and Control A.5.7 — Threat intelligence)**.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors with access to the organization’s information systems, data, or infrastructure.

## 3. Risk Assessment Process
### 3.1 Identification
- Identify information assets, including data, systems, and processes.
- Identify threats and vulnerabilities associated with these assets, including those specific to AI systems.

### 3.2 Evaluation
- Assess the likelihood and impact of identified risks using a risk matrix.
- Prioritize risks based on severity.

### 3.3 Risk Treatment
- Select a treatment option for each high-priority risk: mitigate, accept, avoid, or transfer.
- Assign responsibility for risk treatment to relevant stakeholders.

### 3.4 Review
- Conduct risk assessments annually or after significant changes to the business or threat landscape.
- Document and report findings to senior management.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
### 4.1 ISMS Owner (Owner)
- Ensure resources are allocated for risk assessments and mitigation.
- Oversee the Risk Assessment Team.

### 4.2 Management
- Approve risk treatment plans and provide resources for mitigation.

### 4.3 Risk Assessment Team
- Conduct and document risk assessments.

### 4.4 Employees
- Report potential risks or vulnerabilities to the IT team.

## 5. Compliance
Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contracts.

## 6. Review and Updates
This policy will be reviewed annually or after significant changes to the business or threat landscape.
