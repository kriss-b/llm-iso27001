# Communication Security Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy ensures the secure transmission of information across networks and the protection of information systems from interception or tampering in alignment with **ISO 27001:2022 (Controls A.8.20 – A.8.23)**, which cover network security, security of network services, network segregation, and web filtering.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who transmit or receive information on behalf of the Company.

## 3. Policy Statements
### 3.1 Network Security
- All network traffic must be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
- Wireless networks must use WPA3 encryption and require authentication.
- Remote access must be secured using VPNs with MFA.

### 3.2 Email Security
- Email must be encrypted for sensitive communications (e.g., using PGP or S/MIME).
- Phishing and spam filters must be enabled for all email accounts.
- Employees must not open suspicious attachments or links.

### 3.3 Voice and Video Communications
- Voice and video calls must be encrypted (e.g., using Signal, Zoom with end-to-end encryption).
- Conference call links must not be shared publicly.

### 3.4 Third-Party Communications
- Third parties must comply with the Company’s communication security requirements.
- Data shared with third parties must be encrypted and protected by contractual agreements.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
### 4.1 CTO (Owner)
- Implement and maintain communication security controls.

### 4.2 IT Team
- Support the CTO in implementing communication security controls.

### 4.3 Employees
- Comply with communication security procedures.

### 4.4 Managers
- Ensure teams are aware of communication security risks.

## 5. Compliance
Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contracts.

## 6. Review and Updates
This policy will be reviewed annually or after significant changes to communication technologies or threats.
