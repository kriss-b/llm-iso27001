# AI Policy

## 1. **Purpose**
This AI Policy establishes the framework for the responsible development, deployment, and use of AI systems at **ACME CORP** ("the Company"). It aligns with **ISO 42001:2023 (Clause 5.2 — AI Policy, and Control A.2.2 — AI policy for the organization)** and supports our goal of achieving certification.

---

## 2. **Scope**
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third-party vendors involved in the development, deployment, or use of AI systems.
- All AI systems owned, operated, or procured by the Company, including:
  - AI models developed internally.
  - Third-party AI services and APIs integrated into Company products or operations.
  - AI-assisted tools used by employees to process Company data.

---

## 3. **AI Management Objectives**
| **Objective**                  | **Target**                                      | **Measurement**                              |
|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Responsible AI use             | Zero incidents of harmful or biased AI output  | Incident reports, AI impact assessments     |
| Transparency                   | 100% of deployed AI systems documented         | AI system inventory                         |
| Human oversight                | Human review for all high-stakes AI decisions  | Process audits                              |
| Regulatory compliance          | 100% adherence to EU AI Act and ISO 42001:2023 | Internal and external audits                |
| Employee awareness             | 100% completion of AI safety training          | Training completion records                 |

---

## 4. **Roles and Responsibilities**
| **Role**                 | **Responsibility**                                                                 |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CEO**                  | Ultimate accountability for responsible AI use across the organization.           |
| **CTO**                  | Oversee AI system governance, lifecycle management, and technical controls.       |
| **CISO**                 | Ensure AI systems are included in risk assessments and security controls.         |
| **DPO**                  | Ensure AI systems comply with data protection regulations.                        |

---

## 5. **Policy Statements**
### 5.1 **AI Risk and Impact Assessment**
- All AI systems must undergo an **AI System Impact Assessment** prior to deployment, evaluating potential harms to individuals, groups, and society.
- AI-related risks must be identified, assessed, and treated in accordance with the **[Risk Assessment Framework](../../risk_assessment/risk_assessment_framework.md)**, with AI Specificity classified as `Indirect` or `Direct`.

### 5.2 **AI System Lifecycle**
- AI systems must be managed across their full lifecycle: design, data collection, training, evaluation, deployment, monitoring, and decommissioning.
- Any significant change to an AI system (e.g., model update, fine-tuning, change of AI provider) must follow the change management process.

### 5.3 **Data for AI**
- Data used to train, fine-tune, or evaluate AI systems must be documented, classified, and handled in accordance with the **[Data Classification Policy](../../policies/data_classification_policy.md)**.
- The provenance, quality, and potential biases of training data must be assessed before use.

### 5.4 **Human Oversight**
- AI systems must not make final decisions in high-stakes contexts (e.g., hiring, legal, medical, financial) without human review.
- Automated AI decisions must be logged and auditable.

### 5.5 **Transparency**
- All AI systems in production must be documented in the AI system inventory.
- Users and affected parties must be informed when interacting with or subject to AI systems.

### 5.6 **Fairness and Non-Discrimination**
- AI systems must be evaluated for bias before deployment and monitored for discriminatory outputs during operation.
- Identified bias must be treated as a security incident and remediated promptly.

### 5.7 **Third-Party AI**
- Third-party AI providers must be assessed in accordance with the **[Supplier Security Policy](../../policies/supplier_security_policy.md)**.
- Contracts with AI providers must include data handling, model governance, and incident reporting obligations.

---

## 6. **Training and Awareness**
- All employees must complete AI safety training as part of the annual security awareness programme.
- Employees developing or operating AI systems must receive additional role-specific AI governance training.

---

## 7. **Policy Review**
- This policy must be reviewed **annually** or after significant changes to AI systems, regulations, or the threat landscape.
- Updates must be approved by the **CEO** and communicated to all stakeholders.

---

## 8. **Exceptions**
- Exceptions to this policy must be **documented, approved by the CTO and CISO, and reviewed annually**.

---

## 9. **Enforcement**
- Non-compliance with this policy may result in **disciplinary action**, up to and including termination.
